About AIMS Rwanda

The African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) Rwanda is the fifth Centre of Excellence in the AIMS Global Network. Established in 2016 with the support of the Government of Rwanda, AIMS Rwanda is committed to nurturing Africa’s brightest minds in the mathematical sciences. Since its inception, AIMS Rwanda has graduated over 510 students—38% of whom are women—through its rigorous postgraduate programs, including:

  • A Structured 10-month Research-Oriented Master’s Program
  • An 18-month Co-operative (Co-op) Master’s Program, which integrates academic training with industry experience through work placements

Vision

A prosperous Africa, driven by innovative education and the power of mathematical sciences.

Mission

To empower talented young Africans to become creative leaders in science and technology.

About AIMS Global Network

The African Institute for Mathematical Sciences (AIMS) is a pan-African network of Centres of Excellence for postgraduate training in mathematical sciences, research, and public engagement across Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics (STEM).

Founded in 2003 in South Africa by renowned physicist Prof Neil Turok , the AIMS network has since expanded to include centres in: Senegal, Ghana, Cameroon and Rwanda.

Together, AIMS centres are catalyzing Africa’s transformation by:

  • Advancing innovative scientific training and human capital development
  • Fostering technological innovation and groundbreaking research
  • Promoting scientific literacy and public engagement

The AIMS Model: Reinventing the 21st Century African University

The AIMS model is a pioneering approach to postgraduate education in Africa, characterized by:

  • A pan-African student body
  • A broad and modern curriculum with strong computational and research components
  • A focus on critical thinking and problem-solving
  • Full bursary support, and continuous access to electricity, computing, and internet
  • A bilingual learning environment
  • Close mentorship by full-time tutors and world-class visiting faculty

Our graduates go on to impactful careers in research, industry, education, and public service.

The AIMS model is unique in its pan-African focus, broad curriculum, independent critical thinking, inclusion of modern computational techniques and research components. AIMS identifies top African graduates and provides them with a world class education in mathematical sciences, enabling them to pursue careers in research, industry and civil society. Students benefit from full bursary support, continuous access to computing, internet and electricity, close interaction with full-time tutors and visiting professors, as well as a bilingual education.

Programs at AIMS Rwanda

AIMS Rwanda offers two primary postgraduate programs in Mathematical Sciences:

Structured Master’s Program

This 10-month intensive program blends coursework with research and emphasizes:
  • Advanced mathematical modeling and computing methods
  • Problem-solving and critical thinking
  • Scientific writing and communication
  • Interdisciplinary applications including big data, computer security, and financial mathematics
Program Overview:
  • Semester I – Skills Courses: Foundational courses in mathematics, computing, and physics aimed at building core problem-solving abilities.
  • Semester II – Review Courses: Elective, in-depth courses in fields such as biomathematics, financial mathematics, computer science, and more. Students choose courses in consultation with the Academic Director to ensure a coherent academic path.
  • Semester III – Research Project: Students undertake a three-month research project under expert supervision, culminating in a scientific report and oral defense. Outstanding projects may be suitable for publication or serve as the foundation for further research.

Co-operative (Co-op) Master’s Program

This 18-month program combines academic training with practical industry experience. Developed to bridge the gap between academia and industry, the co-op program equips students with technical and professional skills to transition into the workforce.

Program Highlights:
  • Builds on the structured master’s foundation
  • Includes specialized courses in data science, machine learning, cybersecurity, computational finance, and more
  • Offers a 6-month industry internship with local or international partners
Curriculum Highlights

All programs include:

  • Core academic courses taught by international experts
  • Continuous training in computing, communication, leadership, and entrepreneurship
  • Research supervision and personalized academic mentorship
  • Full scholarship support and access to campus resources

About the Research Chair

AIMS Rwanda holds a Research Centre with Chairs focusing on areas including Data Science, discrete mathematics and probability, and optimization on water system networks. The research centre is well-positioned to lead Africa into the 4th industrial revolution.

It shares facilities with Quantum Leap Africa (QLA) which seeks to prepare Africa for the quantum revolution. QLA attracts students and researchers in the quantum science domain from all over the world and has recruited a Research Chair in Data Science and its applications.

Research at AIMS Rwanda also focuses on fields like climate modelling, plant-interactions, human disease modelling, computational immunology, quantum physics/science, applied mathematics, and Bayesian methodology. Research team members comprise of postdoctoral fellows, visiting researchers, MSc, PhD students and interns.

The centre is ideally positioned to respond to current and emerging trends/challenges such as the COVID-19 pandemic, climate change, and food security. Recently, a COVID-19 pool testing method was developed and has been successfully used by Rwanda and beyond, to track people infected with the SARS-CoV-2 virus, as well as to identify possible asymptomatic people within communities. This algorithm was developed by the AIMS Research team, under the leadership of Prof Wilfred Ndifon, the AIMS Chief Scientific Officer, in partnership with the Rwanda Biomedical Centre (RBC).

Requirements and Eligibility Criteria

We seek motivated students who demonstrate academic excellence and a passion for scientific discovery.

General Requirements

  • Must be a citizen of an African country
  • Age 16-25 years old
  • Strong academic record in science subjects
  • Demonstrated interest in research or scientific innovation
  • English proficiency (TOEFL/IELTS required for non-native speakers)

Required Documents

  • Completed application form
  • Academic transcripts
  • Personal statement (500 words)
  • Two letters of recommendation
  • Research proposal (for research programs)

Application Process

Follow these steps to apply for our programs and become part of the future of African science.

Step 1: Choose Your Program

Review our available programs and select the one that best matches your interests and career goals.

Step 2: Prepare Your Documents

Gather all required documents including transcripts, personal statement, and letters of recommendation.

Step 3: Submit Application

Complete the online application form and upload all supporting documents before the deadline.

Step 4: Interview Process

Selected candidates will be invited for an interview with our admissions committee.

Step 5: Final Decision

Successful applicants will be notified within 4-6 weeks of their interview.
